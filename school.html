About the School

What École nationale de cirque is

École nationale de cirque is a professional circus arts school in Montreal, Quebec. It is known for focused circus training and for being part of one of the strongest circus communities in North America.

Location

Montreal, Quebec, Canada

Montreal is widely recognized for its strong circus culture, which adds to the school’s influence and identity.

Founded

1981

The school has decades of history and has spent years building a serious reputation in circus education.

Programs

Preparatory, secondary, and higher education

ENC offers multiple levels of circus training, including advanced professional preparation.

College experience

Three-year professional diploma path

The college-level path helps define the school as a serious long-term training environment.

Training style

Physical, technical, and artistic

The school’s identity is built around training artists who need strength, discipline, coordination, and expressive performance.

Community

Part of a larger circus network

ENC is strengthened by existing inside a bigger circus ecosystem instead of standing alone.

About stats like acceptance rate, graduation rate, tuition, and housing

College prep facts

Verified official costs for the 2026–2027 Circus Arts program: file opening fee CA$50, annual registration fee CA$200, annual related fees CA$370, Quebec resident tuition CA$5,200, French citizen tuition CA$5,885, non-Quebec Canadian/permanent resident tuition CA$8,550, international tuition CA$16,115, plus CA$815 annual insurance for international students except French citizens. These fees are listed by ENC and marked as subject to change.

Admission basics: applicants must have completed secondary studies or equivalent, have sustained practice in circus or a related discipline, submit a complete application, pass the audition steps, complete French proficiency assessment, and receive confirmation of acceptance.

Audition format: ENC states that the 2026–2027 process includes a mandatory video pre-audition, followed by an invitation-only second stage in person in Montreal or by additional video.

Important note: I did not add an acceptance rate, graduation rate, or housing price because I could not verify those clearly from official sources yet. For a college prep class, it is better to leave those as “not confirmed” than to guess.
