Personal Perspective

Why I Want to Go to ENC

I want to go to École nationale de cirque because it represents the level of training, discipline, and artistic excellence that I want to reach as a circus artist.

World-class training

ENC stands out to me because it is known for serious professional training. I do not just want a place where I can practice circus. I want a school that pushes artists technically, physically, and creatively at a very high level.

Cirque du Soleil goal

One of my biggest goals is to give myself the strongest possible chance of reaching the highest level of performance, including opportunities connected to Cirque du Soleil and other world-class productions. ENC feels like one of the clearest paths toward that kind of future.

My disciplines

The disciplines that matter most to me are partner acro and straps. Both demand strength, trust, control, timing, and discipline. I want to develop those skills in an environment where they are pushed to a much higher level and treated with the seriousness of professional performance training.

What I want to build afterward

My goal is not only to benefit from the training for myself. I want to learn from a place like ENC and then bring that level of knowledge, structure, and artistic ambition back to Boston. Long term, I want to help build something bigger here: a serious circus college and training environment that gives young artists access to stronger coaching, clearer opportunities, and a real path forward in the circus world.

Why this matters to me personally

ENC matters to me because it represents more than a school name. It represents the standard I want to reach. I want the kind of training that changes how I perform, how I think, and what I believe is possible. I want to learn at a level that prepares me for world-class performance, and then use that experience to help create something meaningful beyond myself. Going to ENC would not just be about leaving Boston. It would be about coming back stronger, with more vision, and with the ability to help build a future for circus here that is bigger, more serious, and more lasting than what exists now.
